New starters and P45

Everyone should get a P45 from their last employer when they stop working for them. Your P45 is a record of all your pay and tax taken so far in the current year.

A P45 shows your:

  • Tax code and Pay As You Earn (PAYE) reference number
  • National Insurance number
  • Date you left employment
  • Earnings and tax taken away during the year

We need your P45 details so we can put you on the right tax code. Keep part 1A of your P45 safe for your own records. Part 2 and Part 3 should be posted or emailed to the HR service desk at hrservicedesk@buckinghamshire.gov.uk.

Make sure you keep your original P45 safe, as you wont be able to get another copy. It is proof of your income from the start to the end of your employment.

Starter checklist

If you dont have a P45, you will need to fill out a starter checklist. This might be because:

  • Youre starting your first job
  • Youre working here as a second job at the same time as another one
  • You dont have a P45 for the current tax year

Tax codes

Zero T (0T) tax codes

If you dont have a P45 or you havent filled in a new starter checklist before your first payday, you may be put on a Zero T (0T) tax code. The trouble is that this will probably mean you pay more tax than you should. However, if you do pay too much tax, you may be entitled to get the money back but it may take some time and you may have to contact HMRC directly.

You can find out if youre on a 0T tax code by looking at your payslip.

Changing your tax code

If you find youre on a 0T tax code, make sure you submit your P45 or new starter checklist and quote your individual payroll number which you should find on your last payslip. If you send the documents to HR in time we may be able to amend your tax code before your next payslip goes out. Also, if HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) send you a tax code directly through the post, we won't be able to apply it, we'll need HMRC to contact us directly to tell us what the code is.

The wrong tax code

If HMRC issue an electronic tax code directly to you then there isnt anything we can do to change it. They will only deal with you directly, so youll need to phone them on 0300 200 3300. Once we receive a new tax code directly from HMRC, we will apply it as soon as we can. Depending on when we receive it, it may not be until the following month's pay that you see the change.
